Automated Test Equipment

ATE is computer-controlled instrumentation that applies electrical stimuli to a Device Under Test (DUT) and measures its responses — verifying functionality, performance, and reliability at production speed and volume.

Explore Learning Modules

What is ATE?

Every chip that ships has been tested — often multiple times. ATE systems sit at critical points in the semiconductor lifecycle: wafer sort (testing dies on the wafer), final test (packaged parts), and system-level test (SLT, testing the chip in a near-real system context). Without ATE, defective parts would reach customers, yields would be unknown, and the cost of failure would be enormous.

Why ATE Exists

  • Cost of test: Balance coverage vs. test time per part.
  • Yield tracking: Bin parts and monitor production health.
  • Time-to-market: Catch defects before they reach the field.
  • Volume: Test thousands of parts per hour in production.

Core Architecture

  • Test head: Pin electronics and measurement hardware.
  • DIB / Loadboard: Routes signals to the DUT socket.
  • Handler / Prober: Positions the DUT against pogo pins.
  • Test program: Patterns, levels, timing, and limits.

Types of ATE

Different device classes require specialized test platforms. The major categories below cover most of the semiconductor test market.

SoC / Digital

High-pin-count digital and mixed-signal SoCs. Examples: Advantest V93000, Teradyne UltraFLEX.

Memory

DRAM, NAND, and flash at wafer and final test. Examples: Advantest T5500/T5800 series.

Analog / Mixed-Signal

Precision analog, data converters, and power management ICs. Heritage: Eagle Test Systems ETS series.

RF / Wireless

Cellular, Wi-Fi, and radar device test. Examples: LitePoint (Teradyne), NI PXI-based systems.

Power / Discrete

SiC, GaN, MOSFETs, and IGBTs. Examples: Chroma ATE, Cohu Diamondx.

System-Level Test (SLT)

Tests the chip in a near-real system environment — often the last gate before shipping.

Bench vs. Automated Test

Both bench instruments and ATE measure electrical behavior — but they serve different stages of the product lifecycle.

🔬

Bench Testing

  • Oscilloscopes, SMUs, spectrum analyzers, and custom setups
  • Manual or scripted — high flexibility for exploration
  • Low volume: one DUT at a time, engineer-driven
  • Used for validation, characterization, and debug
  • Lower capital cost, higher cost per part tested
🏭

Automated Test Equipment

  • Dedicated test head with hundreds of pin channels
  • Fully automated via test program — deterministic timing
  • High volume: multisite parallel test with handlers
  • Used for production screening and yield tracking
  • High capital cost, pennies per part at volume

Bench validates the design. ATE screens every part that ships.
